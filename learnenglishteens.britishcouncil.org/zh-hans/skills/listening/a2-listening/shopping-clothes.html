Shopping for clothes

Listen to the conversation between a customer and a shop assistant and do the exercises to practise and improve your listening skills.

Instructions

Do the preparation exercise before you listen. Then do the other exercises to check your understanding.

Preparation

Do this exercise before you listen.

Remote audio URL

Transcript

Shop assistant: Can I help you?
Customer: Yes, have you got this T-shirt in other colours?
Shop assistant: We’ve got it in white, black, red and purple. What size do you want?
Customer: Medium.
Shop assistant: OK, in medium we’ve got black and red.
Customer: And in purple?
Shop assistant: No, just black and red.
Customer: OK, red. Can I try it on?
Shop assistant: Yes, of course. The changing rooms are over there.
(pause)
Shop assistant: Is it OK?
Customer: Yes, I’ll take it.
Shop assistant: That’s £10.95. Would you like to pay by credit card or with cash?
Customer: Cash please. Here’s twenty.
Shop assistant: OK, thanks, that’s nine pounds, 5 p change and here’s your receipt.
Customer: Thanks. Bye.

Check your understanding: grouping

What is the meaning of.............. nine pounds, 5 p change????

Submitted by JoModerator on Wed, 08/05/2020 - 06:59

Hi Sherry4869,

The price of the T-shirt is £10.95. 

The customer wants to pay in cash and gives the shop assistant a £20 note.

The shop assistant gives the customer £9.05 back - this is called 'change' (the money that is returned to someone who has paid for something that costs less than the amount that they gave).

When we speak, we can say 'nine pounds, 5 p' for £9.05.

 

I hope that helps!

Jo (LearnEnglish Teens team)
